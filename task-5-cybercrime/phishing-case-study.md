# Phishing Case Study

## Title: Email Phishing Attack on a Bank Customer

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers attempt to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, and banking details by pretending to be a trusted organization. In this case study, a bank customer received an email that appeared to be from their bank. The email informed the customer that their bank account would be suspended unless they verified their details immediately.

The email included a link that directed the customer to a fake website designed to look exactly like the official bank website. Believing the message to be genuine, the customer entered their login credentials and banking details on the fake page. The attackers captured this information and used it to transfer money from the customer's account without permission.

This incident resulted in financial loss and caused stress and inconvenience for the victim. The customer later reported the incident to the bank and the cybercrime reporting authorities. The bank blocked the compromised account and advised the customer to change all passwords immediately.

This case study highlights the importance of verifying email sources, avoiding suspicious links, and being cautious while sharing sensitive information online. Awareness and careful attention can help prevent phishing attacks and protect personal data.
